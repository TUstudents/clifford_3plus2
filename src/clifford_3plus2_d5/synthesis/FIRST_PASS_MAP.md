# First Pass Map Of The Sidecars

This is the first conceptual pass through the repository. It is not the final
paper text. Its role is to fix the inner map before the detailed sidecar-by-
sidecar synthesis begins.

The working thesis is that the flavor and mass theory has one spine:

$$
\text{carrier} \to \text{boundary transfer} \to
\text{family depth} \to \text{Clebsch readout} \to
\text{radial spectral response}.
$$

CP and killed generation routes sit around this spine as constraints. They are
not optional decoration: they tell us where phases can live and which attractive
generation mechanisms are dead.

## 1. Carrier And Arena

The carrier layer is not yet the flavor mechanism. It is the stage.

The lepton sidecar constructs one Standard Model generation on the chiral
$16$ of Spin(10), represented over a real $32$-dimensional carrier with a
compatible complex structure. The construction uses the Pati-Salam
factorization of $\mathrm{Cl}(0,10)$ and then the conventional breaking

$$
SU(4)\times SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R
\longrightarrow
SU(3)_c\times SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y .
$$

This is a strong carrier construction, but it includes declared structural
choices: the Pati-Salam factorization, the compatible $J$, octonion/Fano
choices, and the hypercharge normalization.

Certainty: `C:7` for the one-generation carrier construction under these
choices. `C:1` for any claim that this layer alone derives three generations.

The spacetime sidecar supplies the Bialynicki-Birula BCC Weyl walk, the Dirac
assembly, gauge-link covariance, Wilson plaquettes, and the numerical
gauge/Higgs/Yukawa arena. This is the dynamical stage on which boundary
response can eventually be tested. It is not yet a forward flavor simulator.

Certainty: `C:7` for the implemented BCC/QCA arena. `C:3` for using it as a
future forward selector of spectral mass data.

The obstruction sidecar matters philosophically: it says that important
carrier structures do not fall out of several primitive microscopic rule
classes. Thus the synthesis must not pretend that the carrier choices have
already been generated by the QCA.

Certainty: `C:8` for the closed primitive obstruction classes. `C:6` for the
broader lesson that the current paper should separate carrier construction
from microscopic carrier derivation.

## 2. Boundary Transfer

The boundary-response sidecar is the first real flavor spine.

The primitive object is a resolved/unresolved split and its Schur complement:

$$
\Sigma(z)=V^\dagger(z-H_Q)^{-1}V .
$$

The silver transfer root is exact:

$$
\epsilon=\sqrt{2}-1 .
$$

In the half-line chain language, the head Weyl function satisfies

$$
m(z)=\frac{1}{z-m(z)}
$$

and at the transfer probe $z=2\sqrt{2}$ gives

$$
m(2\sqrt{2})=\sqrt{2}-1.
$$

This is one of the cleanest theorem-level facts in the repo. It is the
dimensionless flavor root, not the lattice spacing parameter used in CP/SME.

Certainty: `C:9` for the transfer root and the distinction
$\epsilon_{\rm silver}\neq\epsilon_{\rm lattice}$.

The framed neutrino response has the form

$$
K_\nu=\epsilon^2P_u+P_b .
$$

The important synthesis point is that full residual $S_3$ symmetry would make
the response too symmetric. The physical response needs framing: a selected
port reduces the symmetry and makes $P_u$ and $P_b$ meaningful.

Certainty: `C:7` for the framed product-chain response. `C:6` where equal
return normalization or framing assumptions remain load-bearing.

The vacuum selector thread is a separate but related boundary story. It closes
up to the explicit axiom that positive quartic backreaction bounds the selector
radius. This should be treated as a named physical input, not hidden inside the
theorem.

Certainty: `C:6` for the selector closure with the quartic axiom. `C:3` for a
microscopic derivation of that quartic coefficient.

## 3. Unified Flavor Track

The flavor A-track says that flavor structure is read from one boundary through
sector projections:

$$
\Sigma_f(z)=V_f^\dagger(z-H_Q)^{-1}V_f .
$$

The useful content is not a numerical mass derivation. The useful content is
that the quark/lepton sectors share the same transfer root and differ by
quantum-number projections, especially the color label. The theory is
predictive for structure only if the remaining free inputs are counted honestly.

Certainty: `C:7` for shared transfer and sector-projection structure. `C:4`
for the claim that one explicit $H_Q$ on the chiral-16 carrier numerically
reproduces all sector responses, since that is not yet constructed.

The A-track also exposes the key open input:

$$
\text{generation depth embedding } \{0,2,6\}.
$$

The sidecar language sometimes calls this a remaining input. The synthesis
should sharpen that: deriving $\{0,2,6\}$ is the generation problem in transfer
language.

Certainty: `C:8` for this diagnosis as a cross-sidecar conclusion.

## 4. Family Depth

The depth-scar sidecar gives the best mathematical object for the depth
spectrum:

$$
D_{\rm scar}=2\Delta(P_3),
$$

where $P_3$ is the path

$$
u-a-b .
$$

The spectrum is exact:

$$
\operatorname{Spec}D_{\rm scar}=\{0,2,6\}.
$$

The same object has three useful dual descriptions:

$$
\text{path Laplacian}
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\text{length-3 nilpotent flag}
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
S_3\to Z_2 \text{ repair scar}.
$$

This is the first major duality to carry into the paper. It prevents
rediscovery: a new theorem producing the same path flag is not a new depth
mechanism unless it derives the physical origin of the scar.

Certainty: `C:9` for the path spectrum and nilpotent/Laplacian equivalence.
`C:6` for the scar as a conditional physical origin of the hierarchy.

The depth-hop-Walsh sidecar gives the hard negative result. The genuine BB hop
source does not realize the desired cube/parity tower. In invariant language,
an $S_3$-invariant operator on the residual three-port space

$$
3=1\oplus 2
$$

has at most two eigenvalues by Schur's lemma. The three distinct depths
$\{0,2,6\}$ require an $S_3$-breaking spurion.

Certainty: `C:1` for the raw BCC hop/Walsh derivation of the depth skeleton.
`C:8` for the Schur obstruction that an unbroken residual $S_3$ cannot produce
three distinct family depths.

## 5. Scalar Clebsches

The scalar-Clebsch layer must be separated from current amplitudes. This is one
of the main conceptual cleanups.

For the up sector, the primitive object is a length-$3$ nilpotent response:

$$
N^3=0,\qquad
\exp(xN)=I+xN+\frac{x^2}{2}N^2 .
$$

The scalar grade readout is

$$
C_u(x)=\left(\frac{x^2}{2},x,1\right).
$$

With $x=1/\sqrt{2}$ this gives

$$
C_u=\left(\frac14,\frac{1}{\sqrt2},1\right).
$$

Certainty: `C:9` for the Taylor identity. `C:6` for the physical value
$x=1/\sqrt{2}$, since it depends on the two-channel no-leakage theorem being
microscopic rather than merely modeled.

For the down sector, the primitive object is the regular $S_3$ shell:

$$
\mathrm{Reg}(S_3)=1_{\rm triv}\oplus 1_{\rm sign}\oplus 2_{\rm std}\oplus
2_{\rm std}.
$$

The baseline count $(6,2,4)$ is natural. The candidate $(6,2,5)$ is available:
rank $5$ is regular minus a one-dimensional irrep. But availability is not
selection. The missing theorem must choose the standard copy and the excluded
line.

Certainty: `C:7` for availability inside the $S_3$ algebra. `C:5` for the
down candidate as a theory-organizing mass Clebsch. `C:3` for a still-unknown
defect-selection rule.

## 6. Radial Mass Response

The radial-response sidecar is where actual mass values belong.

Finite group theory gives shells, projectors, and depth filters. It does not
force pole positions or residues. The physical mass information is spectral:

$$
\Sigma_f(z)=V_f^\dagger(z-H_{Q,f})^{-1}V_f .
$$

The target textures can be written as positive Stieltjes measures,

$$
\Sigma_f(z)=\sum_i\frac{w_i}{z-\lambda_i},\qquad w_i>0.
$$

This proves compatibility with a boundary bath and gives inverse Jacobi
reconstructions. It does not yet prove that the QCA selects those baths.

Certainty: `C:9` for the Feshbach/Schur identity. `C:4` for target mass
textures as reconstructed spectral measures. `C:3` for any forward
spectral-density principle until proven.

This sidecar also kills a tempting confusion: literal $\exp(N)$ is not the
family-space Yukawa matrix. The nilpotent Taylor object is a scalar-response
jet, not a physical Yukawa mixing matrix.

Certainty: `C:1` for literal nilpotent Yukawa as the physical mass matrix.

## 7. CP, Strong CP, And SME

CP has two structural locations.

First, the BB walk has lattice CP violation localized in the $T_{2g}$ harmonic
at leading lattice order. This is tied to the chiral coin structure and
vanishes in the continuum limit.

Second, graph-native CP in the depth-scar language requires a loop. A tree has
no intrinsic phase: all edge phases can be removed by port rephasings.

Certainty: `C:8` for the CP sidecar's structural slots. `C:9` for the no-phase
statement on a tree.

Strong CP is structurally safe in the current lattice construction: the
$A_{2u}$ theta-like channel is absent in the relevant audits, and direct
lattice-gauge computation confirms the triviality in the tested sectors.

Certainty: `C:8` for strong-CP safety in the audited construction.

The SME sidecar maps the CP-odd $T_{2g}$ lattice correction into dimension-5
SME coefficients. The resulting bound makes the channel currently
unfalsifiable. This is a phenomenological constraint, not a flavor prediction.

Certainty: `C:7` for the mapping; `C:2` for its current usefulness in selecting
flavor theory.

## 8. Killed Generation Routes And Koide

Triality, broken triality, exceptional-algebra candidates, topology, and
anomaly forcing all fail as derivations of three equivalent SM generations.
The common lesson is not merely that those routes failed. It is that generation
structure is not cheaply supplied by the obvious algebraic symmetries.

Certainty: `C:8` for the individual closed routes. `C:6` for the synthesis
lesson that a successful depth derivation must introduce a genuine
family-breaking mechanism.

Koide is different. It is a consistency geometry, not a generation mechanism.
The BCC body diagonal and Koide cone are compatible, but the tested
BCC-$Z_3$ Yukawa locus does not contain the PDG mass triple as a derived point.

Certainty: `C:7` for Koide/BCC consistency. `C:1` for the tested
BCC-$Z_3$ orbit locus as a derivation of the observed charged-lepton masses.

## Detailed Iteration Order

The detailed passes should now proceed in this order:

1. `boundary_response`: extract the transfer theorem, framed response, and
   selector axiom.
2. `flavor_a_track`: compress the unified-boundary claim and isolate the depth
   input.
3. `depth_scar`: prove the $P_3$ Laplacian/nilpotent/scar equivalence in paper
   form.
4. `depth_hop_walsh`: write the killed BCC derivation and Schur obstruction as
   the clean no-go theorem for unbroken $S_3$.
5. `scalar_clebsch`: separate scalar mass Clebsches from current amplitudes and
   state the missing down-selection theorem.
6. `radial_response`: place masses in the spectral measure layer and separate
   reconstruction from forward derivation.
7. `cp`, `strongcp`, `sme`: locate CP and remove fake epsilon correlations.
8. `triality`, `broken_triality`, `exceptional`, `topology`, `koide`: convert
   closed routes into constraints on the final paper narrative.

The first final-document skeleton should emerge after pass 4. At that point the
paper will have its spine: boundary response, transfer root, and depth no-go.
