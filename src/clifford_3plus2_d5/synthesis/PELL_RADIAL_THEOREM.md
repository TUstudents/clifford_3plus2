# Pell Transfer And The Radial Hierarchy

This note synthesizes the third non-sidecar note. It is the strongest current
radial statement, but it also exposes the remaining keystone obstruction.

The main correction is conceptual. The mass hierarchy should use the
contraction ratio of the silver Pell transfer matrix,

$$
\eta=(\sqrt2-1)^2,
$$

not an independently fitted small number. The remaining work is not to tune
$\eta$, but to derive the integer transfer depths and the electroweak
assignment of sectors.

## 1. Pell Transfer Theorem

The silver Pell transfer matrix is

$$
T_{\rm Pell}=
\begin{pmatrix}
2&1\\
1&0
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Its eigenvalues are

$$
\lambda_+=1+\sqrt2,\qquad
\lambda_-=1-\sqrt2.
$$

Therefore

$$
\left|\frac{\lambda_-}{\lambda_+}\right|
=
\frac{\sqrt2-1}{\sqrt2+1}
=
(\sqrt2-1)^2.
$$

Define

$$
\epsilon=\sqrt2-1,\qquad
\eta=\epsilon^2=3-2\sqrt2.
$$

Then $\eta$ is the per-step contraction ratio between the subdominant and
dominant Pell transfer channels. A depth-$k$ radial mode is naturally weighted
by

$$
\eta^k.
$$

This is the forward source of integer transfer powers. The irrational is not
generated by a fit. It is the eigenvalue ratio of an integer matrix.

Certainty: `C:9` for the eigenvalue theorem. `C:7` for reading radial depth as
Pell-channel contraction once $T_{\rm Pell}$ is accepted as the boundary
propagator.

## 4. Closure Lengths: What Is Real

The naive Cayley word-length argument does not give the wanted slopes. In the
Cayley graph of $S_3$ generated by transpositions, a three-cycle has word
length $2$ and a transposition has word length $1$. That gives a $2:1$ ratio,
not the required $3:2$.

Thus the slopes are not Cayley distances.

The useful mechanism is closure length under two different repair rules:

1. A holomorphic triality repair is forward-only. If $\sigma$ is a 3-cycle, the
   repair closes after

   $$
   \sigma^3=e.
   $$

   This gives a closure length $3$.

2. A Hermitian bipartite repair is an out-and-back involution. If $\tau$ is a
   transposition, the repair closes after

   $$
   \tau^2=e.
   $$

   This gives a closure length $2$.

Given the holomorphic/Hermitian repair split, the exponent slopes are therefore
forced:

$$
\hbox{up slope }3,\qquad
\hbox{down slope }2.
$$

Certainty: `C:9` for the closure lengths once the repair rules are assumed.
`C:1` for deriving the $3:2$ slope ratio from ordinary Cayley word length.
`C:5` for using these closure lengths as physical radial depth increments.

## 7. The Electroweak Keystone Obstruction

The note then tries the right hard question: can the flavor group itself force

$$
\hbox{up}=\hbox{holomorphic},\qquad
\hbox{down}=\hbox{Hermitian}?
$$

The answer is no.

The group $S_3$ has one nontrivial normal subgroup,

$$
A_3\triangleleft S_3,
$$

and one corresponding sign grading. This grading can distinguish even
$Z_3$-type moves from odd transpositions. If one maps it to QCA chirality, it
can distinguish chirality-preserving from chirality-flipping structure.

But both up and down Yukawa couplings flip chirality:

$$
Q_L\leftrightarrow u_R,
\qquad
Q_L\leftrightarrow d_R.
$$

The up/down distinction is not chirality. It is weak isospin and Higgs
orientation:

$$
T_{3L}=+\frac12
\quad\hbox{versus}\quad
T_{3L}=-\frac12,
$$

with the Higgs or conjugate Higgs carrying the compensating electroweak charge.
That information lives in

$$
SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y,
$$

not in residual family $S_3$.

Therefore $S_3$ alone cannot derive the up/down holomorphic/Hermitian
assignment. The assignment is an electroweak/Higgs coupling input until the
minimal Higgs-defect bridge is built.

[DYNAMICAL_PREMISE_LEDGER.md](DYNAMICAL_PREMISE_LEDGER.md), sharpens this
further. Hypercharge conservation forces

$$

\qquad
d\text{-type}\leftrightarrow H.
$$

But this is the Standard Model conjugation distinction. It does not force
holomorphic/coherent repair for up and Hermitian/diffusive repair for down.
Thus the remaining bit is dynamical, not merely electroweak representational.

Certainty: `C:9` for the statement that both up and down Yukawas are
