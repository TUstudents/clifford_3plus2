# Spacetime QCA carrier

The `spacetime_qca` sidecar supplies the BCC spacetime carrier and simulation
prototype for the theory. It is the source of the Bialynicki-Birula walk used
by the CP, SME, strong-CP, and depth-hop analyses.

It should be read as a carrier and dynamics scaffold, not as a completed
interacting Standard Model.

## BCC Dirac walk

The load-bearing primitive is the Bialynicki-Birula BCC Weyl automaton. The hop
matrices are pinned to the 1994 source convention, with

$$
q_\pm={1\pm i\over4}.
$$

With Bloch convention $\exp(-i\epsilon k\cdot h)$, the right and left Weyl
blocks have continuum Hamiltonians

$$
H_R(k)=\sigma\cdot k,\qquad
H_L(k)=-\sigma\cdot k.
$$

The chiral Dirac assembly gives

$$
H_D(k)=\alpha\cdot k,
\qquad
\alpha_i=\operatorname{diag}(\sigma_i,-\sigma_i).
$$

This is the Hamiltonian-form continuum audit. C:8, with the all-momentum
unitarity inherited from the pinned source convention rather than reproved
symbolically in the sidecar.

The current implementation tensors the spacetime Dirac factor with the exact
real internal carrier:

$$
\mathbb C^4_{\rm Dirac}\otimes\mathbb R^{32}_{\rm internal}.
$$

Conceptually, after choosing the compatible $J$ basis, this is

$$
\mathbb C^4_{\rm Dirac}\otimes\mathbb C^{16}_{\rm internal}.
$$

The compressed $\mathbb C^{16}$ implementation is not yet the matrix
representation used by the code. C:7.

## Lorentz recovery

The free finite-spacing audit compares the BCC trace-cosine diagnostic to
$
\cos(\epsilon |k|).
$
The two Weyl blocks individually have cubic anisotropy with opposite signs, so
the chiral Dirac assembly cancels that cubic term. The BCC Dirac residual begins
at quartic order:

$$
\Delta_{\rm BCC\,Dirac}
={\epsilon^4\over6}
\left(k_x^2k_y^2+k_x^2k_z^2+k_y^2k_z^2\right).
$$

The naive hypercube control has lower-order anisotropy:

$$
E_{\rm cube}^2-|k|^2
=-{\epsilon^2\over3}(k_x^4+k_y^4+k_z^4)+O(\epsilon^4),
$$

and has the expected sampled corner doublers. C:8 for this free-dispersion
diagnostic.

This does not prove full Lorentz invariance, boost covariance, interacting
renormalization, or all-momentum no-doubling in the fundamental BCC Brillouin
zone. C:4 for full continuum QFT recovery.
