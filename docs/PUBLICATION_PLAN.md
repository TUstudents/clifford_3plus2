# Publication Plan — Obstruction Classification for the Carrier-First Spin(10) Bridge from QCA Rule Data

Status: writing-plan v1. Targets a single self-contained paper on the trunk's obstruction-classification result.

## Context — what the paper is and is not

**What it is**: a classification of structural obstructions to deriving the chiral-16 of Spin(10) carrier (with its `(3,2)` real coarse split, complex structure `J`, and Standard Model commutant) from microscopic finite-depth real-orthogonal QCA rule data on `R^10`. The paper presents five propositions, four with rigorous proofs and one (4a) as an exact finite computational census, together with two structural corollaries closing the on-site bridge class.

**What it is not**:
- not a derivation of the Standard Model from QCA;
- not a result about the Cl(0,10)/lepton sidecar (which uses declared algebraic input, a different question);
- not a no-go theorem about all chiral QCAs (the propositions close specified mechanism classes, not arbitrary rules).

The paper's claim is precise: *within the explored primitive classes, the carrier-first Spin(10) bridge cannot be derived from microscopic rule data*. The "explored primitive classes" are explicitly defined and the unscanned classes are explicitly named as open.

This is the right framing because it matches what was proved.

## Audience and venue

Primary audience: theoretical physicists and mathematicians interested in QCA constructions of relativistic/internal symmetry structures (Bialynicki-Birula, Meyer, Arrighi, D'Ariano, Furey, Hastings/Po lineages).

## What the paper proves

Six numbered results, each reproducible from the repo:

1. **Proposition 1** (E1/E2 coarse primitive obstruction). In the bounded primitive class generated by block-blind primitives (global clock, whole-block reflections, within-block permutations) on `R^10`, no rule generates an unseeded complementary rank-`(6,4)` central idempotent pair without producing rank-one center distinguishers, which are forbidden by no-locking.
   - Type: structural proof with exhaustion witness over 170 finite-depth words across 10 primitive sets.
   - Reproducibility: `uv run python scripts/explore_rule_space.py --check`.

2. **Proposition 2** (commuting second-layer no-locking obstruction). For commuting block-preserving `U, V ∈ O(10)` with `V` non-scalar on a coarse block, `V` has a spectral projector `Q ≼ P_α` of rank strictly less than 6 that is central in `R⟨U, V⟩`, violating no-locking.
   - Type: structural proof (Bezout polynomial decomposition + commutativity of the generated algebra).
   - Witness: trivial; the proof is constructive.

3. **Proposition 3** (Floquet-α-type block-preserving noncommuting locking obstruction). For block-preserving noncommuting `U_1, U_2 ∈ O(10)` with `U_1` of Floquet-α type (one irreducible quadratic factor per coarse block), if `A_R = R⟨U_1, U_2⟩` contains a central `J` with `J² = -I`, then `A_R` either has a lower-rank central idempotent inside a coarse block (no-locking violated), has full internal addressability `M_3(C)` or `M_2(C)` on a block (SM commutant violated), or is block-commutative (the noncommuting assumption fails).
   - Type: structural proof (Wedderburn on `A_R|_{coarse block}` with `J ∈ Z`).
   - Witness: exhaustive signed-twist scan of 3840 candidates, reduced to 96 symmetry classes, 720 generated-J hits, 240 minimal four-J hits, **0** in the no-locking shape.
   - Reproducibility: `uv run python scripts/floquet_alpha.py exhaust-signed-twists --check`.

4. **Corollary 3.1** (on-site bridge closure). On a single ten-dimensional on-site carrier, the Floquet-α block-preserving class cannot simultaneously satisfy: (i) coarse rank-`(6,4)` center, (ii) geometric gate algebra in `C P_α ⊕ C P_β`, (iii) rule-generated `J` unique up to global `±` gauge.
   - Type: structural consequence of Propositions 1, 2, 3.

5. **Proposition 4a** (projector-free monomial-hop Bloch obstruction, finite census). Over a complete brute-force enumeration of `2400` projector-free monomial-hop 1D Bloch candidates with windings `(4, 4, 4, 3, 3)`, every closed coarse-center candidate (`1320` total) has `Z(A_R)` admitting only the rank profile `[0, 4, 6, 10]` with split-real projected centralizers `R^3` (rank-6 block) and `R` (rank-4 block); the split-center solver returns `(solved, ())` for compatible `J`, certifying solved-empty rather than solver-failed.
   - Type: exact finite computational census, not asymptotic claim.
   - Witness: `data/scans/bloch_path_a_monomial_center_full_summary.json` with 10 batch summaries; 2400 candidates; all closed at algebra dimension 34.
   - Reproducibility: `uv run python scripts/bloch_path_a_stepwise.py --max-candidates 2400 --check`.

6. **Conditional Lemma 4b.1** (split-real projected centralizer obstruction). If `B_P` is a split real commutative semisimple algebra, then `B_P` contains no real orthogonal complex structure on `P R^10`.
   - Type: structural proof (one paragraph: characters of `R^m` cannot square to `-1`).
   - This is the lemma that lifts 4a from "finite census" to "structural" if combined with a structural proof that all coprime monomial hops produce split-real projected centralizers (the open Proposition 4b).

7. **Proposition 5** (Route-1 SM-inequivalence of compatible J orbits). The four compatible orthogonal complex structures of the Route-1 noncommuting signed-twist algebra split into two SM-inequivalent orbits under independent (α, η) block-sign flips; the η-flip sends `N_2 → 2 - N_2` and does not preserve the hypercharge `Y = -N_3/3 + N_2/2`. Therefore the strict `{±J}` standard cannot be relaxed to a fixed-`SU(3) × SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y` gauge-equivalence class.
   - Type: structural proof (Hodge complement on the η block changes hypercharge).
   - Witness: `scripts/gauge_equivalence_check.py` returns `fixed_sm_branching_tables_match_mod_global_pm = false`.
   - Scope: fixed-SM relaxation only; charge-conjugation / outer-automorphism extension is explicitly out of scope.

**Open**:

- **Conjectural Proposition 4b** (coprime monomial-hop incompatibility). Every projector-free coprime-winding monomial-hop rule producing the coarse `(6,4)` center has split-real projected compatible centralizers. Census of 1320 closed candidates supports this; the structural proof for the general class is open.

The paper presents 4b as a conjecture with finite witness, with Lemma 4b.1 as the load-bearing implication.

## Paper structure (target ~25 pages)

### Title
*Five Obstructions to Carrier-First Spin(10) Bridge Derivation from Quantum Cellular Automaton Rule Data*

### Abstract (~200 words)
- Set the question: can a finite-depth real-orthogonal QCA rule on `R^10` derive the chiral-16 of Spin(10) carrier, the `(3,2)` real coarse split, a compatible complex structure `J`, and the Standard Model commutant — without declaring any of them?
- State what's proved: five obstructions over the explored primitive classes (block-blind, commuting locks, Floquet-α noncommuting on-site, monomial-hop Bloch, gauge-equivalence relaxation).
- Frame the result: the carrier-first program cannot succeed in these classes; the unscanned classes (non-translation-invariant rules with full intertwiner search, higher-dimensional coarse-graining, parameterized rule families) remain open.
- Methodology note: all obstructions are exact-symbolic; finite witnesses are reproducible from a public repository at a documented git revision.

### 1. Introduction (~3 pages)
- The Spin(10) GUT carrier and Standard Model branching.
- The carrier-first principle: derive `J`, `(P_α, P_β)`, and commutant from rule data, not by declaration.
- The QCA framework (Schumacher-Werner / Arrighi / D'Ariano lineage), real-orthogonal rules, finite-depth locality.
- This paper's specific question: which mechanism classes admit a rule-derived bridge?
- This paper's specific answer: five obstruction classes are closed; the unscanned classes are named.

### 2. Setup and definitions (~3 pages)
- The real carrier `R^10`, the candidate ansatz `K_x = R^2_clock ⊗ R^5_mode` with `J = ε ⊗ I_5` and `P_α/P_β = I_2 ⊗ diag(...)`.
- The verdict contract: rule-generated central `J`, no-locking guardrail, SM commutant requirement.
- Primitive classes: block-blind, commuting, noncommuting block-preserving, Bloch monomial-hop, wall.
- The `rule_to_verdict` machinery (briefly; the load-bearing math is the propositions, not the infrastructure).

### 3. Proposition 1: block-blind primitives (~2 pages)
- Statement and proof (the proof is the Wedderburn-on-symmetric-primitives argument plus exhaustion).
- Witness: E1/E2 sprint over 170 finite-depth words.
- Reproducibility one-liner.

### 4. Proposition 2: commuting locks (~2 pages)
- Statement and proof (Bezout on minimal polynomial + commutativity).
- Discussion: why this closes the "add a commuting lock" repair attempt.

### 5. Proposition 3 and Corollary 3.1: Floquet-α noncommuting on-site (~5 pages)
- This is the central proposition. Statement, Wedderburn proof in detail.
- Three structural cases: lower-rank center, full addressability, block-commutativity.
- Finite witness: 3840 → 96 classes → 720 J-hits → 240 minimal-J → 0 in no-locking shape.
- Corollary 3.1: on-site bridge closure under the Floquet-α-type hypothesis.
- Discuss what 3.1 does and does not close (non-Floquet-α hypotheses still open in principle).

### 6. Proposition 4a and Conditional Lemma 4b.1: monomial-hop Bloch (~5 pages)
- Statement and finite-census witness (2400 candidates exhausted).
- Conditional Lemma 4b.1 proof (one-paragraph: characters of `R^m` can't square to -1).
- Conjectural Proposition 4b stated with explicit hypothesis (coprime winding numbers, projector-free coefficient algebra, split-real projected centralizer invariant).
- Why the conjecture is plausible (12 verified candidates have split-real centralizers; the structural extension is open).

### 7. Proposition 5: SM-inequivalence of compatible-J orbits (~3 pages)
- Statement and proof (hypercharge Hodge-complement under η-flip).
- Witness (gauge_equivalence_check).
- Scope statement: fixed-SU(3) × SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y only; the outer-automorphism / charge-conjugation extension is open.
- Why this rules out the "gauge-equivalence relaxation" repair.

### 8. Open mechanism classes (~2 pages)
- Non-translation-invariant rules: the R^12 wall lab tested one specific construction (Session 8 report; center too large). Other wall mechanisms with distinct intertwiner classes remain open.
- Parameterized rule families with Berry-phase or Chern-number invariants.
- Higher-dimensional carriers with coarse-graining to R^10.
- Polynomial-hop Bloch (Proposition 4b structural proof still open).

### 9. Discussion (~2 pages)
- Relation to the framework-first approach (Cl(0,10) sidecar): same target, opposite methodological commitment. The sidecar accepts declared structure; this paper's results say the declared structure cannot be derived.
- Relation to Bialynicki-Birula / D'Ariano lineage: those works derive Dirac kinematics from QCA principles. This work asks whether the *internal* SM carrier admits an analogous derivation; the answer in the explored classes is no.
- What a positive answer would require: a primitive class not closed by the five propositions.

### 10. Conclusion (~1 page)
- Restate the five propositions as a coherent classification.
- The open classes as the next research target.
- Honest framing: this is a no-go in specified classes, not a no-go theorem in full generality.

## Honest framing of the result

The paper claims:

1. The five propositions are *real obstruction theorems*, valid in their stated primitive classes.
2. Proposition 4a is a *finite exact computational census*, not an asymptotic numerical claim.
3. Conjectural 4b is *honestly conjectural*, with a clear path to making it a theorem (prove the split-real-projected-centralizer invariant).
4. The unscanned classes are *open*, not implicitly closed.
5. The paper does not claim to derive the SM, to disprove all QCA-derivations, or to settle the framework-first question.

What the paper does NOT claim:
- That the Standard Model is unreachable from QCA in general.
- That the Cl(0,10) framework-first sidecar is wrong (it's a different question).
- That the obstructions extend to dimension other than 10 or to carriers other than `R^10`.

## What this paper enables and forecloses

**Enables**: A clear statement of what the carrier-first QCA-derivation program has and has not achieved. A reference for future workers attempting QCA-derivations of SM-like structures to know which mechanism classes are closed. A baseline for the framework-first lepton sidecar (the natural companion paper).

**Forecloses**: Further trunk investment in the closed primitive classes. The unscanned classes (defects, parameterized families, higher-dim coarse-graining) are open but require different infrastructure; they are research projects, not extensions of the present paper.

## Risk assessment

**Main risks for the obstruction paper**:

- *Reviewer pushback on "no-go in specified classes" framing*. Mitigation: be very precise about scope. Every proposition's hypothesis must be stated explicitly. The five obstructions are not a single theorem; they are a classification.

- *Reviewer asks for Proposition 4b proof*. Mitigation: present 4b as conjectural with finite witness. The conditional Lemma 4b.1 is the load-bearing piece and is structurally proved.

- *Reviewer asks why the unscanned classes weren't scanned*. Mitigation: the open-mechanism section explicitly names them, explains why each requires different infrastructure, and frames them as future work.

- *Reviewer questions whether the obstructions are "structural" vs "computational"*. Mitigation: distinguish carefully. Propositions 1, 2, 3 are structural (Wedderburn-style proofs). Proposition 4a is a finite census (exact, not asymptotic). 4b is conjectural. Proposition 5 is structural (Hodge complement). The paper labels each accordingly.

**Low risks**:
- Reproducibility (the repository is the supplementary material; any reviewer can `uv run` the scans).
- Mathematical correctness (the propositions are independently checkable from the proofs).
- Scope creep (the paper is one self-contained classification).

## Single-paragraph mission statement

This paper presents a classification of five structural obstructions to deriving the carrier-first Spin(10) bridge from QCA rule data on `R^10`. Each obstruction is proved or witnessed in a specified primitive class; together they close the on-site bridge problem and substantially constrain the Bloch-symbol bridge problem. The unscanned mechanism classes are explicitly identified. The paper does not claim that QCA cannot derive the Standard Model; it claims that the specific carrier-first methodology cannot succeed in the explored primitive classes, and identifies the open classes as the natural next research direction.
